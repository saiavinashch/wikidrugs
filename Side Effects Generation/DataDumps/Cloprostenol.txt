'''Cloprostenol''' is a synthetic analogue of [[Prostaglandin F2alpha|prostoglandin F<sub>2α</sub>]] (PGF<sub>2α</sub>).<ref name="Cooper 1981"/> It is a potent luteolytic agent; this means that, within hours of administration, it causes the [[corpus luteum]] to stop production of [[progesterone]], and to reduce in size over several days.<ref name="Cooper 1981">{{cite journal|last1=Cooper|first1=M.|title=Prostaglandins in veterinary practice|journal=In Practice|date=1 January 1981|volume=3|issue=1|pages=30–34|doi=10.1136/inpract.3.1.30}}</ref> This effect is used in animals to induce [[estrus]] and to cause abortion.<ref name="Plumb 2015">{{cite book|last1=Plumb|first1=DC|title=Plumb's Veterinary Drug Handbook|date=2015|publisher=Wiley|isbn=9781118911938|edition=8th|chapter=Cloprostenol Sodium}}</ref>
