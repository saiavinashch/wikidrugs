'''Boudreaux's Butt Paste''' is an American brand of [[Moisturizer|skin cream]] that started out as a remedy for [[diaper rash]].<ref name=babybottonline>{{cite web|last=Lee|first=Daniel|title='Butt Paste' improves baby's bottom line|url=http://www.wkyc.com/news/story.aspx?storyid=53164|publisher=[[WKYC]]|date=June 5, 2006|accessdate=March 3, 2013}}</ref><ref name="PtbaM">{{cite book|last=Spinks|first=Tom|title=Prepare to Be a Millionaire|year=2008|publisher=HCI|isbn=0757307140|pages=112â€“116|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=nlRLXLzYjxoC&pg=PA112&dq=%22Boudreaux's+Butt+Paste%22&hl=en&sa=X&ei=P_0yUdmRIcPz0gG8rIHQBQ&ved=0CEEQ6AEwAQ#v=onepage&q=%22Boudreaux's%20Butt%20Paste%22&f=false}}</ref> It has also been used for the treatment of various [[dermatology|skin ailments]] including [[psoriasis]], [[jock itch]], [[shingles]],  [[cold sore]]s and [[acne]].<ref name=telegraphco>{{cite web|title=No ifs and buts about this paste|last=Whitworth|first=Melissa|url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/health/children_shealth/3311507/No-ifs-and-buts-about-this-paste.html|publisher=[[The Daily Telegraph]]|date=November 1, 2004|accessdate=March 3, 2013}}</ref><ref name="Sayre"/> 

The product was created in the 1970s by George Boudreaux of [[Covington, Louisiana]], while he was working as an intern pharmacist.<ref name="PtbaM" /> He continued to work on the formula after becoming a licensed pharmacist and sold it at his pharmacy, later naming it "Boudreaux's Butt Paste" after a physician told him a story about a patient who had referred to the product as such.<ref name="PtbaM" />

Boudreaux began distributing the product more extensively after he sold his pharmacy in 1974.<ref>{{cite news|title=Boudreaux's Butt Paste goes national|newspaper=The Daily Reveille|date=April 1, 2004}}</ref> Manufacturing moved from [[Alabama]] to [[New Orleans]] in 2004.<ref name="Sayre">{{cite web|last=Sayre|first=Alan|title=Butt Paste: The name says it all|url=http://www.spokesman.com/stories/2004/aug/24/butt-paste-the-name-says-it-all/|publisher=[[Associated Press]] in [[The Spokesman-Review]]|date=August 4, 2004|accessdate=March 3, 2013}}</ref> After [[Oprah Winfrey]] recommended the product on [[The Oprah Winfrey Show|her show]] the response was so great that the company's website crashed,<ref name=telegraphco/> and demand for Boudreaux's Butt Paste quadrupled.<ref name=indianaeconomicdigest/> When [[Brad Pitt]] talked of a "horrible diaper rash" in two of his children,<ref name=people-rash>{{cite news|title=Brad Pitt Needs Your Help! Got Any Diaper Rash Advice?|url=http://stylenews.peoplestylewatch.com/2006/09/11/brad-pitt/|accessdate=March 4, 2013|newspaper=People|date=September 9, 2006}}</ref>  a ''[[People (magazine)|People]]'' magazine article asked readers for advice. It reported that in under five hours almost 900 readers responded, recommending Boudreaux's Butt Paste "overwhelmingly".<ref name=people-overwhelming>{{cite web|title=Brad's Diaper Drama: Readers to the Rash-cue|url=http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,1533962,00.html|publisher=[[People (magazine)|People]]|date=June 12, 2006|accessdate=March 3, 2013}}</ref>

In August 2005, a {{convert|14|ft|adj=on}} tide caused by [[Hurricane Katrina]] hit the area where Boudreaux's Butt Paste was manufactured, so emergency production was moved to Indiana.<ref name=babybottonline/> Soon after, the company was sold to Blairex Industries in [[Columbus, Indiana]]. After the sale, Boudreaux continued to work in Covington to work on marketing and developing new products based upon Butt Paste. Blairex focused on manufacturing and distribution.<ref name=indianaeconomicdigest>{{cite news|last=Johannesen|first=Kirk|title=Columbus-based Blairex Laboratories grows with Boudreaux merger|url=http://www.indianaeconomicdigest.net/main.asp?SectionID=31&SubSectionID=62&ArticleID=27155|accessdate=March 3, 2013|newspaper=[[The Republic (Columbus)|The Republic]] ''reprinted in'' Indiana Economic Digest|date=May 28, 2006}}</ref> 

On December 29, 2011, Blairex sold the brand to C.B. Fleet Company, Inc.<ref>{{cite web|title=Company Overview of Blairex Laboratories, Inc., Boudreaux's Butt Paste|url=http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/private/snapshot.asp?privcapId=214230976|publisher=Business Week|accessdate=March 3, 2013}}</ref> 

The brand became a [[NASCAR]] sponsor beginning with a [[Junie Donlavey]]-owned car  driven by [[Kevin Ray]] in the [[Sprint Cup Series|Nextel Cup Series]]<ref>{{cite news | first = Chris | last = Jenkins | title = Bottom line: Edgy sponsor name, OK'd by NASCAR | date = May 26, 2004 | url = http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/sports/motor/nascar/2004-05-26-edgy-sponsor_x.htm | work = [[USA Today]] | accessdate = March 3, 2013}}</ref> and [[Kim Crosby]]'s #24 Butt Paste Chevrolet, run by [[GIC-Mixon Motorsports]] in the [[Nationwide Series|Busch Series]].<ref name="Sayre"/><ref>{{cite web|last=Hill|first=Jemele|authorlink=Jemele Hill|title=Butt Paste Is No Joke For This Driver|url=http://articles.orlandosentinel.com/2005-04-10/sports/0504100264_1_nascar-driver-boudreaux-busch|publisher=[[Orlando Sentinel]]|date=April 10, 2005|accessdate=March 3, 2013}}</ref> A NASCAR spokesman said to ''[[USA Today]]'': "I think it's very fitting that Junie Donlavey, who has brought more drivers into the world of NASCAR than any other owner, is now being sponsored by a baby product."<ref name=chicagotribune>{{cite news|last=Redeye Sports Staff|title=NASCAR sponsor eases burn|url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2004-05-28/news/0405290103_1_junie-donlavey-dover-international-speedway-nascar|accessdate=March 3, 2013|newspaper=Chicago Tribune|date=May 28, 2004}}</ref>  

In 2008, due to Boudreaux's Butt Paste ads on the car, Ray's No. 90 Ford was named the fifth scariest NASCAR paint scheme of all time by ''[[ESPN The Magazine]]'' for having "'BUTT PASTE' slapped on the rear quarter panels in giant red lettering and a cartoon baby riding on the hood."<ref>McGee, Ryan. (November 1, 2008) "[http://sports.espn.go.com/rpm/nascar/cup/columns/story?columnist=mcgee_ryan&id=3670751 Halloween got you spooked? Get a load of these monsters]". ''[[ESPN The Magazine]]''. Accessed March 3, 2013.</ref>

== Ingredients ==
The ingredients are:
*16 percent [[zinc oxide]] (Original, other versions have more)
*a proprietary blend of [[Balsam of Peru|Peruvian balsam]]
*[[castor oil]]
*[[mineral oil]]
*[[Paraffin wax|Paraffin]]
*[[Petrolatum]]
